About Me

I am a recent computer science graduate from the University of Massachusetts Boston. My primary interest is the intersection between technology and healthcare. It is my firm belief that state-of-the-art healthcare should be available to all, and software development is one of the best ways to accomplish this. This passion of mine lead me to a 10 month collaboration with NYU Langone's Comprehensive Epilepsy Center, where I helped developed N-Tools-Browser, a visualization tool for electrocorticography data. A paper summarizing our work can be found here.

Aside from healthcare, I have a wide range of interests including finance, artificial intelligence, and supply chain logistics. I hope to join a team where I can have measurable real-world impact and grow my skills as a developer. Whether I am designing an intuitive user-interface, performing data analytics, or optimizing back-end servers, I will be happy to make an impact in any way I can.
